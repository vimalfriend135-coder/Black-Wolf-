How To spot Malicious URLs

  • Check the Domain Structure: Malicious URLs use subdomains (e.g., paypal.com.verify-login.net) to fool you. The true domain is the word immediately preceding the first slash (e.g., verify-login.net).
  • Watch for Typosquatting: Subtle typos or character replacement are common (e.g., googIe.com with a capital "I" instead of an "l", or faceb00k.com).
  • Look for HTTPS: Safe sites use encryption. However, modern phishing sites also have SSL certificates, so look beyond the padlock symbol!

Use Lengthy Passphrases

Combine 4 or more random non-related words (e.g. correcthorsebatterystaple). This increases the character combinations index exponentially.

Always toggle on Hardware tokens or Authenticator apps. It invalidates the absolute efficacy of credentials-only hacks.

What is Steganography?

Unlike Cryptography, which scrambles a message to make it unreadable, Steganography hides the very existence of the communication. By embedding secret payloads into seemingly innocent carrier files, an actor can transmit sensitive data in plain sight.

🖼️ Image Steganography Payloads are embedded inside pixel structures—most commonly by altering the Least Significant Bits (LSB) of color channels.
🎵 Audio Steganography Secrets are masked inside audio frequencies, echo delay profiles, or phase offsets that cannot be picked up by human hearing.
📹 Video Steganography Combines both audio and visual steganography across high-capacity, temporal frame sequences to conceal large files.
📄 Text Steganography Hides messages using trailing whitespaces, zero-width characters, or semantic structure manipulations of sentences.
